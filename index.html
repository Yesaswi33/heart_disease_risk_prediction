Input Field Descriptions

Age: The age of the individual. The model uses this to understand how age impacts heart disease risk. Typical range is from 20 to 80 years.

Sex: The gender of the individual, where '1' represents Male and '0' represents Female. This factor is used to account for gender-related risk factors in heart disease.

Cigarettes Per Day: The number of cigarettes an individual smokes daily. Smoking is a major risk factor for heart disease, and this input helps to evaluate smoking-related risks.

Total Cholesterol: The total cholesterol level in the blood. High cholesterol levels are linked to increased heart disease risk. Typical values range from 150 to 250 mg/dL.

Systolic Blood Pressure: The pressure in your arteries when your heart beats. Higher systolic blood pressure indicates a higher risk for heart disease.

Glucose Level: The amount of glucose (sugar) in the blood. High glucose levels are often associated with diabetes, which increases the risk of heart disease.
